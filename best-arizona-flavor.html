Best Arizona Flavor: All the Arizona Flavors, Ranked

February 2024 · 10 minute read

Finding the best Arizona flavor wasn’t easy, and that’s coming from someone who tastes things for a living. First of all, there are a lot of flavors. For this taste test, I sat down to two dozen hulking 23-ounce cans of sugary, gas station teas and fruit juice bevs, which is a daunting experience in itself. Secondly, most of these taste pretty f***ing good. In my youth, when I didn’t care how much sugar I consumed and spent a lot more time popping into convenience stores because I smoked a pack of grits every couple of days, I drank a good amount of Arizona. In 2001, I was incapable of passing up 23 ounces of green tea (with ginseng) or grapeade for a mere 99 cents. Now I am old and fusty, and drinking 50-plus grams of sugar in one sitting isn’t something I do unless I’m feeling self-destructive. Or, you know, I’m being paid to. 

When I set out to taste and rank the best Arizona tea flavors, it came down to both flavor accuracy and overall drinkability. I wanted the teas to taste like tea, and the ades to taste like the fruit they were ade-ing. Flavors that were inaccurate, unremarkable, or inscrutable lost points. I know there’s a whole fandom around Arizona, so I’m curious what you people (hi) think of this ranking of the best Arizona flavors. Feel free to comment, but remember that being hopped up on sugar isn’t an excuse to be agro.
