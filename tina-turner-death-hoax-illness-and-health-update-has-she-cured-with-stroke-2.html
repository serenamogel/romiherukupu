Singer and actress Tina Turner were born in the United States of America. Turner became well-known as the lead singer of the Ike and Tina Turner Revue before beginning a lucrative solo career, earning her the title “Queen of Rock ‘n’ Roll.”

In 1957, Tina made her professional debut with Ike Turner’s Kings of Rhythm. In addition, Turner made her debut on her first album, “Boxtop,” as Little Ann in 1958.

Tina made her debut as Tina Turner in 1960 with the popular duet song “A Fool in Love.” Ike & Tina Turner rose to the status of “one of history’s most fearsome live performances.”

Tina began “one of the biggest comebacks in music history” in the 1980s. What’s Love Got to Do with It, which won the Grammy Award for Record of the Year and became her first and only number-one song on the Billboard Hot 100, was featured on her multi-platinum 1984 album Private Dancer.

Turner was instated into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame twice. Once in 1991 with Ike Turner and again in 2021 as a solo performer. She also won the Kennedy Center Honors and the Women of the Year award in 2005.
